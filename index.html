Reniscience Education LLP is an education consulting firm born out of a desire to promote learning that is empowering, joyful and relevant to the 21st century learner. We work with schools and other educational organizations to provide expertise, interventions and solutions for teacher professional development, whole-school reform, educational leadership development, student learning and curriculum development. We operate by collaborating very closely with our partners, with on-going feedback and support through the needs-assessment, design, implementation and exit phases of every project. All of our efforts are driven by our core beliefs about teaching and learning and focused on our goal of promoting 21st century learning.

WHAT WE BELEIVE

We believe that all teachers are capable of teaching in a manner that is empowering, joyful and relevant to today’s learner, no matter their constraints. Our goal is to encourage and support these efforts at every level- classroom, school and curriculum.

By empowering, We mean practices that foster choice and voice, that free the learner from ONLY one way of knowing, that create an emotionally safe culture, that support meaning-making, that allow learners to feel successful and bring a high level of engagement through the learning process.

All of our work- be it with students, teachers, school leaders or the curriculum- is guided by our core beliefs about education, and rooted in a social justice framework. If you are considering working with us, we would like you to be familiar with our underlying assumptions and working philosophy.

  • Learning is a social, meaning-making process that involves higher order thinking skills; it is also a personal journey that relies on making deep connections to individual experiences, knowledge and belief systems.
  • One of the aims of education must be to develop the capacity to create positive, sustainable change i.e., develop agency. The purpose of education is NOT to learn how to do well on tests.
  • The process of learning must reflect the aims of education. The two should be aligned.
  • A safe, free and adaptive school culture is indispensable to supporting learners who are empowered and can take ownership of their own learning.
  • The purpose of professional development is to promote praxis- a process of critical reflection, (using theory) and intentional action in order to transform one’s practice and oneself.
  • Every learner is unique and has their own distinctive interests, preferences, talents and skills. The learning process must be joyful, relevant and empowering for every learner.

We offer several science workshops and non-science related programs for students 8 years and older. Our science workshops use an inquiry approach, where we carefully guide students to “discover” important science concepts. In the process, students develop key 21st century skills- collaboration, critical thinking, communication and creativity.


Every Reniscience Education science workshop includes the following four elements:

In addition to our science workshops, we offer several unique programs specifically for 7th-10th standard students:


Money$en$e- A financial literacy program for high school students. The goal of this program is to inculcate a sense of financial responsibility, to increase students’ awareness of how money works in the world, to equip them with the knowledge and skills to manage money and to give them an opportunity to run an entrepreneurial business.

FutureKraft- This is a three month long career awareness program for 9th or 10th standard students. It can tailored to include taking an aptitude test, participating in workshops to identify personal strengths and areas of growth, knowing college cutoffs, exploring various career options, receiving one-on-one counseling for the test report, organizing a career fair and a guiding students for a final presentation. This program is run in close collaboration with the partner school or organization.

InFluence- This is a critical media literacy and information fluency program for 8th-10th standard students. Through this program, students explore various types of media including print, broadcast and internet. Using a variety of engaging activities, students build competencies to analyze, evaluate and create messages in a variety of media genres and formats. This program focuses on developing three 21st century skills- critical thinking, creativity and information fluency.

Call2College- This program is especially designed for transitioning 10th standard students who have a million questions about college life, but are afraid to ask. The four components of this program include, study skills, peer pressure & fitting in, identity and an open ended component that is tailored to adolescent students’ specific questions and needs. Developed under the guidance of a counselor and an educator who has worked with adolescents for many years, this is THE program to support you graduating class to not just survive, but thrive in college.

Bioimicry- This week-long series of workshops explore how human technologies can be inspired by designs found in nature. Students understand the connection between design and function, they visit an outdoor location to find designs in nature that fulfil specific functions, they brainstorm solutions for particular problems and they create prototypes based on designs found during their field trip. This is a highly collaborative program, pushing students to work together and rely on each other’s talents

STEM Connection

Every Reniscience Education Science workshop always include a connection to Technology, Engineering or Mathematics (STEM). These connections naturally manifest themselves through the inquiry process. For example, students need to sketch their designs and understand the elements of a design before building a working model of a machine- an engineering skill. Handling a variety of materials allows students to learn about the properties of different materials and find the most suitable one for a particular task- again, a skill that engineers are expected to be proficient at. Students see how everyday technologies like their mobile phones can be used to collect data through simple apps like the stopwatch, camera, voice recorder, etc. Organizing and analyzing quantitative data require students to calculate averages, plot graphs, derive formulae, all of which are skills we usually learn in math.

Collaborative Inquiry

We believe that learning is a social, meaning-making process, and so students in our workshops work in collaborative groups. In every workshop, students are given a challenge - a task they need to accomplish or question they need to answer- that requires them to work together to find the solution or the answer by conducting an inquiry. The inquiry process can take on many forms - students may design and conduct an experiment, sketch and build a model, collect and analyze data. Depending on the age of students, the topic and the challenge, the inquiry may be open, guided or structured.

Instructional Scaffolds

An instructional scaffold is any kind of temporary support that allows a student to move towards deeper understanding and conceptual clarity. Since we want our students to develop their problem-solving capacity, we try to interfere as little as possible in the collaborative inquiry process. However, we do need to provide certain instructional scaffolds along the way to guide the learning and reflection process. Our workshop design always includes deliberate and thoughtful scaffolds in the form of worksheets, organizers, instructions, videos, modelling, demonstrations, questioning, explanations and enough time to reflect. The type and amount of scaffolding used depends on the age of students, the topic and the challenge presented.

Optimal Use of Resources

The materials used in our workshops are typically recycled from everyday objects we find lying around our homes and offices- dried up pen skeletons, bottle caps, boxes and packaging materials, etc. In an effort to develop sustainable workshops, we try to minimize our use of materials that are expensive, bad for our planet or not easily accessible by most students. If we do use such materials, we try to recover them at the end of the workshop, so that they may be reused. This is an important component of our workshop design because we want students to be able to use resources that are easily available to them at home to become creative problem-solvers.

Connecting Theory & Practice

Connecting Theory and Practice: Since we believe strongly in the idea of praxis, our PD programs almost always include a theoretical component that teachers explore through a combination of reading, dialogue and experiential learning. Educational theory cannot be ignored because it allows teachers to:

  • See the big picture and explore the reasons and benefits of employing best practices.
  • Understand how children learn.
  • Investigate and build upon the nature of the subject they teach, to strengthen content.
  • Ensure that their practice becomes more intentional as they begin to function from a place of comprehension rather than default.
  • Share a common language and framework to discuss and resolve problems of practice.
  • Stay abreast of the latest research and trends in education and model continuous learning.
  • Function as a community of professionals with a deeper appreciation of their profession.

development

Critical Reflection

Using a theoretical framework, we push teachers to reflect critically on their own practice. This is an essential step for professional growth and development, and the most difficult. Honestly examining one’s strengths and gaps as a practitioner allows one to purposefully move ahead with a focus of improving teaching and learning. Such critical reflection needs a supportive environment where the focus is on growth rather evaluation. Creating a safe culture is a hallmark of our PD programs.

professional

development

Modelling Best Practices

We practice what we preach and model the most effective practices in our teacher workshops- in the way we set up our ‘class’ culture, deliver content, plan lessons, create evaluations, apply theory, respond to feedback, structure dialogue, use reading strategies, make thinking visible, use scaffolds, etc.

If needed, we also conduct model lessons with students to demonstrate best practices in an authentic context. The last thing we want to do is stand in front of a group of teachers and deliver a lecture on how to engage learners. Our aim is always, to design our PD workshops to be empowering, joyful and relevant to the teachers.

development

Responsive to Needs

We do not have a standardized PD program. While we do have a list of topics (from past PD programs) that school leaders and teachers can refer to, we prefer to have a dialogue with the school team, and if possible, visit the school to better understand the real needs of the community. This allows us to design a program that is responsive to your specific needs and that will be most useful and relevant to you. Thus, while the design of the program is quite consistent in terms it’s design, the learning objectives, pace, resources and follow up can be different for each partnership.

We strongly recommend some sort of follow up and coaching in between or after our workshops.

To get a copy of topics from our past PD programs, please do email us.

Curriculum

We work with schools and other educational institutes to aid K-10 curriculum development. We have the expertise to develop the following components of a strong curriculum.

  • Organize the scope and sequence of learning standards in one grade or across grade levels.
  • Unpack standards to develop learning objectives.
  • Design formative and summative assessments and rubrics for assessments. We specialize in designing project and problem-based assessments and in developing rubrics for 21s century skills.
  • Create lesson plans based on a given set of learning objectives.

When it comes to planning, we are influenced by the “Understanding by Design” (UbD) framework first proposed by Grant Wiggins and Jay Mctighe.

To view a sample of a lesson plan or assessment created by us, please email us.

CITY AS
LAB

City as Lab is our flagship research and inquiry project for 6th-9th standard students.


The project is facilitated by school teachers with Reniscience Education providing on-going support in the form of professional development, lesson plans, on-going feedback, checklists, rubrics and coaching.


Our vision with this program is to build a network of institutions that support authentic research and inquiry in all classrooms across the country.


By participating in this one-of-a-kind project:

  1. Children develop key 21st century skills of collaboration, critical thinking and information fluency.
  2. Teachers become proficient in Project Based Learning
  3. Students get exposure to authentic research skills and develop an inquiry temperament.
  4. Student get excited about writing and learn to write in context with a definite purpose and for a specific audience

“Not everybody's going to be an entrepreneur, but everybody should be financially literate. Financial literacy is a base requirement like spelling or reading or something of the sort that everybody should acquire at any early age. The financial habits you develop when you are young are going to go with you into your adulthood. But you can't be an entrepreneur unless you're financially literate.” – Warren Buffett


Inspired by Mr. Buffett and his ‘Secret Millionaire’s Club,’ Reniscience Education offers a semester long financial literacy program called Money$en$e for 7th and 8th standard students. This program has four modules that focus on:

1. Overview of money

2. Understanding personal finances

3. Business and entrepreneurship

4. Banking, credit cards and investing


Using a combination of field trips, project-based learning, videos, workbooks and several experiential activities, the lessons are specifically designed for this age group. Students are provided with an opportunity to analyze their own finances, create budgets, pitch a business idea to win seed funding from Reniscience Education and then implement their business ideas to earn profit. They visit the RBI monetary museum in Mumbai, interview their parents, apply math concepts learned in class, discuss big ideas like integrity and debt; in the process they develop key 21st century skills of critical thinking, collaboration, creativity and managing complexity.
